- [[relative-synonymous-codon-usage]] ‚Äî RSCU (Sharp & Li 1986) codon-usage-bias measure: n_i¬∑x/Œ£x normalization, 1.0=no bias, bounded [0,n_i], Œ£-over-family=n_i invariant; pooled counts, sense codons only; base anchor of the codon-usage family (vs CAI's max-normalization).
- [[codon-adaptation-index]] ‚Äî CAI (Sharp & Li 1987) whole-gene codon-bias / expression-proxy score in [0,1]: geometric mean of relative adaptiveness w=f/max_synonym_f, low-value-sensitive, stop excluded; dual include/exclude-Met/Trp mode (exclude can give L=0‚Üí0); E. coli oracles; Seqeron deviation = 1e-6 zero-freq clamp (vs Sharp & Li's 0.5 reference-table pseudocount); one level above RSCU.
- [[rare-codon-analysis]] ‚Äî codon-usage family's *localization/diagnostic* unit (`FindRareCodons` + cluster addendum): a codon is rare when its per-family frequency < threshold (default 0.15), reports position/AA/frequency (E. coli AGA 0.04/AGG 0.02/CGA 0.06/CUA 0.04); addendum adds two published cluster methods ‚Äî Clarke & Clark 2008 %MinMax (18-codon window, signed %Min/%Max ‚àà[‚àí100,+100], rare clusters = negative peaks) and Chartier 2012 Sherlocc (7-codon window, ‚â•4 of 7 freq‚â§threshold "slow" positions = a rare-codon cluster); single-codon-AA contributes 0 to %MinMax (no NaN), overlapping windows merged; feeds [[codon-optimization]]'s AvoidRareCodons; oracles ‚àí86.36/+100/+36.47% and 7√óAGA‚Üí1 cluster; deviations None.
- [[codon-usage-comparison]] ‚Äî codon-usage family's *raw* end (`CalculateCodonUsage` + `CompareCodonUsage`): unnormalized codon-count table (non-overlapping triplets, T‚ÜíU, drop partial codon, Dictionary<codon,int>, Œ£counts=total invariant) + Total Variation Distance similarity `1‚àíŒ£|f‚ÇÅ‚àíf‚ÇÇ|/2 ‚àà [0,1]` between two codon-frequency distributions; proven identity 1.0 / symmetry / range [0,1] / disjoint‚Üí0 / partial-overlap-exact properties; empty‚Üí0, TVD-not-cosine deliberate metric choice; Kazusa-verified organism tables; shares RSCU's counting primitive, the distribution-comparison op none of the bias measures provide.
- [[codon-optimization]] ‚Äî codon-usage family's CDS-*rewriting* operation (`OptimizeSequence`): synonymous substitution to improve host expression; five strategies (MaximizeCAI / BalancedOptimization 40-60% GC / HarmonizeExpression=Mignon 2018 / AvoidRareCodons / MinimizeSecondary‚ÜíReduceSecondaryStructure); invariants protein-preservation + Met/AUG & Trp/UGG fixed + stop preserved + CAI‚àà(0,1]; RNA notation, T‚ÜíU, trim-to-codon, 1e-6 CAI clamp; E. coli/yeast/human preferred-codon fixtures; actuator to RSCU/CAI/ENC's measurement.

- [[codon-usage-statistics]] ‚Äî the codon-usage family's *aggregation/reporting* view (CODON-STATS-001, `CodonUsageAnalyzer.GetStatistics` + `CalculateCai`): one pass bundles CodonCounts, RSCU, ENC, TotalCodons and a **positional-GC block** into a `CodonUsageStatistics` record. - [[overrepresented-kmer-discovery]] ‚Äî Motif-Discovery de-novo unit (MOTIF-DISCOVER-001, `MotifFinder.DiscoverMotifs`): finds **unknown** over-represented motifs by enumerating every length-`k` k-mer, counting overlapping occurrences, and ranking by observed/expected enrichment `Count/((N‚àík+1)/4^k)` under a zero-order i.i.d. uniform background (Compeau & Pevzner); deterministic exact single-pass, 0-based positions; `ATGC` in `ATGCATGCATGC` k=4 ‚Üí 768/9‚âà85.333, `AAA` in `AAAAAAAAAA` k=3 ‚Üí 64.0 oracles; `k>N`‚Üíempty, minCount presentation-only, zero-order-only + no-p-value simplifications; the de-novo inverse of the exact [[known-motif-search]] and distinct from the [[consensus-from-alignment]] plurality consensus.
